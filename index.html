IMU-to-4D

Anonymous submission

We propose a novel method to reconstruct 4D human-scene from wearable IMUs time-series input.

Abstract

Understanding human activities and their surrounding environments typically relies on visual perception, yet cameras pose persistent challenges in privacy, safety, energy efficiency, and scalability. We explore an alternative paradigm—4D perception without vision—that reconstructs human motion and 3D scene layouts purely from everyday wearable sensors. We introduce IMU-to-4D, a framework that repurposes large language models for non-visual spatiotemporal understanding of human–scene dynamics. IMU-to-4D takes as input only a few inertial sensors commonly embedded in smart earphones, watches, or phones, and predicts detailed 4D human motion along with the coarse structure of the surrounding scene. Our method models the joint distribution of sequential motion signals and natural languages with a large language model, enabling cross-modal reasoning about geometry, dynamics, and semantics. Experiments across diverse human–scene interaction datasets demonstrate that IMU-to-4D achieves more coherent and temporally stable reconstructions than cascaded pipelines (IMU → pose → scene). Our results show that wearable motion sensors alone can enable rich, privacy-preserving 4D understanding of humans and their surroundings—without capturing a single image.
